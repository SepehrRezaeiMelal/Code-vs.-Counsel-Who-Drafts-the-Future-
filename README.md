# Code vs. Counsel: Who Drafts the Future? A Comparative Study Revealing the Quality Gap between AI-Generated and Human-Drafted Legal Contracts

The recent proliferation of generative artificial intelligence (GAI) has catalyzed profound transformations within the legal landscape, particularly concerning the mechanics of contract drafting. This study presents a rigorous empirical evaluation of the viability of replacing human expertise with artificial intelligence (AI) in this highly specialized domain. Through a functional comparison approach, the research evaluates a legal contract generated by a large language model (LLM) against one drafted by a human expert. The evaluation employs a two-phase methodology conducted by an expert panel of legal practitioners, including 12 judges, 114 lawyers, and 24 judicial experts. To ensure objectivity, the initial phase utilized a blind review—where the authorship of each contract was strictly concealed—to assess the drafted agreements across fundamental dimensions, including legal language quality, content accuracy, structural organization, and statutory compliance. Results from this blind evaluation demonstrated a clear, definitive preference for the human-drafted contract across all metrics against the AI-generated contract. In the subsequent phase, the true authorship of the contracts was explicitly revealed to the panel to capture their subjective attitudes toward AI adoption. Despite the observable performance gap, the findings reveal a divided yet balanced stance, reflecting a cautious and measured willingness to utilize AI for contract drafting. Ultimately, this investigation concludes that AI is currently positioned as a supplementary, efficiency-enhancing tool rather than an autonomous substitute for human legal expertise.
